## I. THE FUNDAMENTAL PROBLEM

### What Super-Extensive Scaling Means

In statistical mechanics, extensive quantities scale as N (or L^d in d dimensions). This is **not optional**:

- **Thermodynamic limit must exist:** lim_{N→∞} E/N = finite
- **Intensive variables must be well-defined:** T, P, μ independent of system size
- **Statistical mechanics requires it:** Partition function Z ~ e^{-βF}, F ~ N
- **Phase transitions require it:** Order parameters, critical phenomena

**If E ~ L⁴ instead of E ~ L²:** 
- No thermodynamic limit
- Energy per particle diverges  
- Not physically reasonable for bulk matter
- Something is fundamentally broken

### This Isn't Just a Technical Detail

The super-extensive scaling isn't a minor bug - it's telling us something deep about the framework itself.

## III. THE DEEP LESSON: LOCALITY MUST BE BUILT IN

### Why the Ising Model Fails

The Poisson equation ∇²Φ = s is **infinitely non-local**:
- Φ(x) depends on s(x') everywhere
- No light-cone structure
- No causal propagation
- Instantaneous action at a distance

In Fourier space: Φ_k = -s_k/k²

For k → 0 (long wavelength):
- Φ_k ~ 1/k² → diverges!
- No suppression of long-wavelength modes
- System can organize at ANY scale
- Nothing prevents λ → L

**This is the root problem.**

### What Real Quantum Systems Have

**Local dynamics:**
- Schrödinger equation: iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = -ℏ²∇²ψ/2m + V(x)ψ
- Locality: ψ(x,t) depends only on ψ(x',t) near x
- Causality: Light-cone structure
- Kinetic term: ∇² operator with correct sign!

**Intrinsic scales:**
- Compton wavelength: λ_C = h/mc
- Healing length: ξ ~ √(ℏ²/m|μ|)
- Domain wall width: w ~ √(stiffness/potential)

**These scales PREVENT super-extensive behavior.**

### The Critical Insight

**For classical behavior to be well-defined (extensive), the substrate MUST have:**

1. ✅ **Kinetic energy structure:** ∇² term with stiffness
2. ✅ **Intrinsic length scales:** Set by kinetic/potential balance  
3. ✅ **Local dynamics:** Finite speed of propagation
4. ✅ **UV cutoff:** Lattice spacing or equivalent

**The Ising model has NONE of these.**

Therefore: **Ising + defrag is TOO SIMPLE to be a realistic substrate.**
